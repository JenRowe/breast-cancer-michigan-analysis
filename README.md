# Breast Cancer Mortality Disparities in Michigan

## Overview
Breast cancer mortality varies significantly across Michigan counties despite consistently high screening rates. This project analyzes county-level data to identify the factors driving these disparities.

## Key Insight
Screening rates do not explain mortality differences. Outcomes are more strongly associated with post-screening factors such as access to care, socioeconomic conditions, and population risk.

## Methods
- Exploratory data analysis
- Correlation analysis (Pearson)
- Composite scoring using standardized mortality rate and total deaths

## Priority Counties
Wayne, Oakland, Macomb, Luce, and Lake were identified as highest priority based on a combined measure of mortality rate and total deaths.

These represent:
- High-burden urban counties (Wayne, Oakland, Macomb)
- High-rate rural counties (Luce, Lake)

## Key Recommendation
Focus on improving post-screening care pathways, including timely diagnosis, treatment access, and follow-up care, rather than expanding screening alone.
